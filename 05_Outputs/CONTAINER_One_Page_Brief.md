# CONTAINER One-Page Brief

Date: 2026-05-14

## Concept

CONTAINER is an early-stage lunar infrastructure concept for a regolith-ballasted construction cell. It lands relatively light, fills itself with local lunar regolith to create working mass, anchors itself, builds landing pads and berms with a precision gantry, then dumps the ballast into useful infrastructure and relocates light.

## Why It Matters

Lunar construction is hard because low gravity reduces traction, stability, drilling reaction force, and compaction force. Launching a permanently massive machine is expensive. CONTAINER separates launch mass from working mass by using local regolith first as ballast and then as construction material.

## Current Baseline

| Parameter | Baseline |
|---|---:|
| Primary mission | 14 m cargo landing pad plus berm |
| Target lander | 45 to 50 metric ton cargo class |
| Berm | About 1.2 m high, about 5 m stand-off |
| Ballast bin | 70 m3, 12 cells |
| Ballast mass | About 110,000 kg regolith |
| Lunar ballast weight | About 178 kN |
| Anchors | 8 helical anchors, about 0.4 m helix, about 1 m length |
| Intake | Blade, 0.5 m auger, enclosed conveyor, spreader |
| Fill target | 3 kg/s, about 10.2 h to fill |
| Gantry | Elevated X-Y-Z system, +/-2 mm target |
| Pad surface | About 154 tiles, each 1 m x 1 m x 0.3 m |
| Power concept | 20 kW solar, 30 kWh battery, 40 kW FSP scenario |
| Full-rate energy | About 380 kWh/day |

## Phase 1 Findings

- Ballast-only design friction capacity is about 71.3 kN after applying a static safety factor.
- Against a 36 kN lateral load assumption, ballast-only safety factor is about 1.98.
- Anchors are mission-critical, not optional.
- Solar-only operation does not support the same 380 kWh/day full-rate cadence.
- 30 kWh battery storage misses a 48 h survival case at 1 kW survival load by about 18 kWh.
- Sintering energy, dust durability, anchor behavior, and fill throughput are the highest-value early tests.

## Credible Positioning

CONTAINER should be presented as a consolidated early-stage engineering baseline pending prototype validation, not as a final or fully proven design.
