# CONTAINER One-Page Brief v2

Date: 2026-06-11

## Concept

CONTAINER is an early-stage lunar construction machine built around a large open-top regolith container body. The revised v2 machine is an 8 m x 6 m x 4 m rectangular bin with a front sliding-lift excavation bucket, four identical long-reach robotic arms on side rails, distributed cameras/sensors, shielded body panels, and a protected underfloor battery/avionics deck accessible from the rear.

The earlier gantry-and-anchor architecture is retired in v2. The machine no longer uses an elevated X-Y-Z gantry, helical anchors, stabilizer pads, outriggers, or a closed top.

## Why It Matters

Lunar construction is hard because low gravity reduces traction, stability, excavation reaction force, and compaction force. Launching a permanently massive construction machine is expensive. CONTAINER keeps the original principle of moving light and working heavy by collecting local regolith into the machine body, using that mass during work, and then placing or dumping the regolith into useful site infrastructure.

| Protection | Radiation and thermal shield panels over fixed body surfaces |

## What Changed From v1

- The precision gantry is removed.
- Helical anchors are removed.
- Stabilizers/outriggers are removed.
- The main body is now explicitly 8 m x 6 m x 4 m and open at the top.
- The old blade-auger-conveyor intake is replaced by a front toothed bucket.
- The bucket moves left-right, up-down, and through curl/tilt.
- Four identical robotic arms move along the 8 m side rails and work both inside and outside the container.
- Arm tips include docking hands so two arms can connect for cooperative handling.
- Batteries and avionics move into a protected underfloor bay with rear access.
- The design includes at least 40 sensors/cameras; the current v2 manifest defines 48.
- Radiation and thermal protection are treated as body-wide shield panels with functional openings.

## Highest-Priority Engineering Questions

- Can the machine remain stable without anchors or stabilizers under bucket loads, arm reach, slope, and partial-fill cases?
- What are the real excavation, lift, curl, and rail loads for the front bucket?
- How heavy and power-hungry are four long-reach side-rail arms?
- Can the underfloor battery/avionics deck be cooled and serviced through the rear hatch?
- What mobility system can support the 8 m x 6 m body across lunar regolith?
- Does the v2 bucket/arm architecture still support the full pad/berm construction sequence, or should the first demonstrator focus on regolith handling?

## Credible Positioning

CONTAINER v2 should be presented as a revised early-stage engineering baseline, not a final manufacturing drawing. The current technical blueprint communicates the architecture and placement logic; fabrication release still requires detailed mechanical design, FEA, actuator sizing, thermal analysis, mass model, materials selection, and prototype testing.
