# CONTAINER Pitch Deck Outline

Date: 2026-05-14

This is a content outline for a future 10 to 12 slide deck. It keeps the story sharp while avoiding overclaiming.

## Slide 1: CONTAINER

Regolith-ballasted lunar construction cell for landing pads and berms.

## Slide 2: The Lunar Construction Problem

- Low gravity reduces traction and reaction force.
- Dust and plume ejecta threaten nearby assets.
- Heavy construction machines are expensive to launch.
- Landing pads and berms are early infrastructure priorities.

## Slide 3: Core Insight

Launch mass and working mass do not need to be the same. CONTAINER lands light, fills locally, works heavy, then turns ballast into infrastructure.

## Slide 4: Mission Baseline

- 14 m cargo landing pad.
- 1.2 m berm at about 5 m stand-off.
- South-polar site.
- 45 to 50 ton cargo lander class.

## Slide 5: System Architecture

- 12-cell ballast bin.
- Blade-auger-conveyor intake.
- 8 helical anchors.
- Elevated precision gantry.
- Modular construction tooling.
- Hybrid solar, battery, and FSP power scenario.

## Slide 6: Operating Cycle

Land, deploy, fill, anchor, compact, sinter, finish, build berm, dump ballast, relocate, repeat.

## Slide 7: Current Numbers

## Slide 8: Phase 1 Model Findings

- Ballast-only safety factor about 1.98 against current lateral load assumption.
- Fill time about 10.2 h at 3 kg/s.
- Tile-field sintering energy about 1,232 kWh at 8 kWh/tile.
- Solar-only does not support full-rate cadence.
- Battery shadow survival needs refinement.

## Slide 9: Highest Risks

- Sintering energy and throughput.
- Dust durability.
- Tile cracking and plume erosion.
- Anchor capacity.
- System complexity.

## Slide 10: Validation Roadmap

- Intake prototype.
- Anchor pull-out test.
- Sintering coupon tests.
- Dust endurance tests.
- Integrated bench demonstrator.

## Slide 11: What We Need Next

- Technical reviewers.
- Lab or university collaboration.
- Regolith simulant access.
- Prototype budget.
- Power/thermal expertise.

## Slide 12: Bottom Line

CONTAINER is promising because it uses lunar regolith twice: first as working mass, then as infrastructure. The next step is evidence.
