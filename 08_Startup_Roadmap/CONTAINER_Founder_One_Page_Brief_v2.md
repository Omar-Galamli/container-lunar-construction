# CONTAINER Founder One-Page Brief v2

Date: 2026-06-15
Status: Startup-safe working brief for revised architecture

## What CONTAINER Is

CONTAINER v2 is an early-stage concept for an open-top lunar regolith construction machine. It is designed to land relatively light, collect local regolith into a large 8 m x 6 m x 4 m container body, use that carried material as working mass, and support landing pad and berm construction with a front sliding-lift bucket and four long-reach side-rail robotic arms.

The core idea is simple: move light, work heavy.

## What Changed From v1

The earlier gantry-and-anchor architecture is retired. The current v2 baseline does not use an elevated X-Y-Z gantry, helical anchors, stabilizer pads, outriggers, a closed top, or the old blade-auger-conveyor intake.

## Why It Matters

Future lunar operations will need prepared surface infrastructure. Repeated landings can eject high-speed regolith, damage nearby assets, and make surface operations harder. A repeatable way to prepare landing pads and berms using local material could become an important part of lunar base preparation.

## Beachhead Application

The first target is not general lunar construction. The first target is a specific infrastructure job:

- Hardened cargo landing pad.
- Protective berm.
- South-polar lunar site assumption.
- 45 to 50 metric ton cargo lander class.

## Current Baseline v2

The current working baseline includes:

- 14 m usable-diameter landing pad reference target.
- About 1.2 m protective berm at about 5 m stand-off.
- 8 m x 6 m x 4 m open-top rectangular container body.
- Front toothed bucket with lateral slide, vertical lift, and curl/dump motion.
- Four identical long-reach robotic arms, two per side rail.
- Arm docking hands for cooperative handling.
- Protected underfloor battery/avionics deck with rear service access.
- Distributed cameras/sensors; the current reference manifest defines 48 locations.
- Radiation and thermal shield panels over fixed body surfaces.

These are baseline assumptions and design targets, not final proven performance.

## What Is Strong Today

- The system-level insight is clear: local regolith can serve first as temporary machine mass and later as infrastructure material.
- The mission is focused: landing pad plus berm, not every possible lunar construction task.
- The v2 architecture removes several complex v1 elements and makes the new assumptions explicit.
- The project has a current baseline, requirements document, assumptions register, risk register, v2 one-page brief, technical blueprint, startup roadmap, and experiment roadmap.

## What Is Not Proven Yet

The highest-risk open questions are:

- No-anchor stability under bucket, arm, slope, braking, and partial-fill cases.
- Bucket excavation, lift, curl, and dump loads.
- Usable carried regolith volume and center-of-mass behavior.
- Side-rail arm reach, stiffness, payload, power, and dust tolerance.
- Arm docking/coupler reliability.
- Dust durability of mechanisms, sensors, radiators, and rear hatch seals.
- Battery and thermal survival during shadow periods.
- Mobility and ground pressure for the large body.
- Pad/berm construction method without gantry tooling.
- Defensible cost and launch-mass model.

## Next 6 Months

- Build the v2 mass, volume, and center-of-mass model.
