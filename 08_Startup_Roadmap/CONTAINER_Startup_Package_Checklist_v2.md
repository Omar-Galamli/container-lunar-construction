## Pitch Deck Structure

1. Problem: lunar landings damage surface assets without prepared infrastructure.
2. Beachhead: cargo landing pads and berms for lunar surface operations.
3. Insight: move light, work heavy using local regolith as carried working mass.
4. Solution: v2 open-top CONTAINER with front bucket and four side-rail arms.
5. Current baseline: body, bucket, arms, sensors, shield panels, underfloor service bay, power assumptions.
6. Evidence so far: v2 baseline, requirements, risk register, technical blueprint, known open assumptions.
7. Biggest unknowns: no-anchor stability, bucket loads, arm stiffness, dust, thermal, mobility, cost.
8. Experiment roadmap: what will be tested first and why.
9. Startup path: advisors, subsystem tests, demonstrator, funding milestones.
10. Ask: advisors, lab access, grant/incubator support, technical collaborators.

## Investor/Advisor Memo Structure

- What CONTAINER v2 is.
- Why lunar landing pads and berms matter.
- Why local regolith as carried working mass is the key insight.
- What changed from v1.
- What is known today.
- What is not proven yet.
- What the next 6 months will prove.
- What help is needed.

## Pre-Pitch Readiness Gate

Before broad external pitching:

- Remove final-design wording.
- Remove or label old gantry/anchor/intake claims as v1 historical material.
- Update cost language to avoid unsupported savings claims.
- Show the top v2 unknowns openly.
- Have at least one advisor-reviewed technical brief.
- Have at least one v2 experiment plan with measurable outputs.
