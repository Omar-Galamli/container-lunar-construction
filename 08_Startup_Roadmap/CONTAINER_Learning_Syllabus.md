# CONTAINER Founder Learning Syllabus

Date: 2026-06-01
Status: Working syllabus

## Purpose

The goal is not to learn everything before building. The goal is to learn enough, in the right order, to ask better questions, avoid weak claims, design useful tests, and talk credibly with advisors.

Every learning block should produce at least one project artifact:

- A note.
- A model update.
- A new advisor question.
- A test idea.
- A design change.
- A removed or downgraded claim.

## Month 0-1: Lunar Environment and Mission Constraints

Learn:

- Lunar gravity, vacuum, thermal cycling, radiation, dust, and illumination.
- South-polar site constraints.
- Communication delay and supervised autonomy.
- Cargo lander operations and plume effects at a high level.

Outputs:

- One-page lunar environment notes.
- List of constraints that directly affect CONTAINER.
- Questions for a lunar surface systems advisor.

## Month 1-2: Regolith Mechanics and Excavation

Learn:

- Lunar regolith density, cohesion, friction angle, grain size, compaction, and variability.
- Excavation, conveying, jamming, wear, and simulant limits.
- Earth-test scaling problems for lunar gravity.

Outputs:

- Regolith assumptions table.
- Intake test variables: simulant type, moisture control, density, slope, rocks, throughput, and jam behavior.
- Anchor test variables: depth, helix diameter, torque, density, slope, and pull-out load.

## Month 2-3: Landing Pad, Berm, and Plume Interaction Research

Learn:

- Why lunar landing pads matter.
- Plume erosion, ejecta, and berm functions.
- Sintered regolith, tiles, joints, cracks, and thermal cycling.

Outputs:

- Tile and berm evidence note.
- Sintering test plan outline.
- List of credible external papers or labs to ask for feedback.

## Month 3-4: Robotics, Gantries, Control, and Autonomy

Learn:

- Gantry stiffness, repeatability, backlash, dust protection, metrology, and thermal expansion.
- Supervised autonomy, safe-stop modes, and fault handling.
- Why continuous teleoperation is not enough for precision lunar work.

Outputs:

- Gantry accuracy risk note.
- Autonomy functions list.
- First safe-stop mode table.

## Month 4-5: Power, Batteries, FSP, and Thermal Survival

Learn:

- Solar power at lunar poles.
- Battery sizing and survival loads.
- Fission surface power as a programmatic dependency.
- Thermal rejection during high-power operations.

Outputs:

- Shadow survival model update.
- Power architecture trade table.
- Questions for power/thermal advisor.

## Month 5-6: Dust Mitigation and Mechanism Durability

Learn:

- Dust adhesion, abrasion, seal design, bearings, rails, radiators, optics, connectors, and electrodynamic dust shields.
- Dust-test limits on Earth.
- Maintenance and inspection modes.

Outputs:

- Dust risk matrix.
- Dust durability test plan outline.
- List of exposed mechanisms to simplify or protect.

## Month 6-7: Space Systems Engineering and Requirements

Learn:

- Requirements, verification, validation, margins, TRL, interface control, and configuration control.
- How to separate mission requirements from design assumptions.

Outputs:

- Requirements cleanup notes.
- Verification matrix update.
- TRL estimate by subsystem.

## Month 7-8: Startup Fundraising, Grants, IP, and Technical Storytelling

Learn:

- Space startup funding paths.
- Non-dilutive grants and competitions.
- Advisor and lab partnership strategy.
- Basic IP, prior art, confidentiality, and defensible claims.

Outputs:

- Funding target list.
- IP/prior-art question list.
- One-page investor/advisor memo draft.
- Pitch deck revision notes.

## Weekly Rhythm

- 3 focused study sessions.
- 1 project artifact update.
- 1 advisor question added to the question bank.
- 1 claim checked against the evidence system.

## Rule

Learning is only complete when it changes the project.
