# CONTAINER Phase 0 Summary v2

Date: 2026-06-11

## What Is Frozen for v2

The current revised baseline uses the new open-top CONTAINER architecture:

- Main body outer envelope: 8 m length x 6 m width x 4 m height.
- Main body is an open-top rectangular regolith container/bin.
- No roof, lid, or closed top over the regolith bay.
- Raised internal floor at about Z900 mm.
- Protected underfloor equipment bay for batteries, avionics, power distribution, cooling manifolds, and connectors.
- Rear shielded service hatch for battery and avionics access.
- Front toothed loader/excavator-style bucket.
- Bucket moves left-right across the front, up-down on vertical lift, and curl/tilt for scoop/dump.
- Bucket reference size: about 2.0 m wide x 1.1 m deep x 1.0 m high.
- Four identical long-reach robotic arms.
- Two arms ride on the left 8 m side rail and two arms ride on the right 8 m side rail.
- Arms work both inside and outside the container.
- Arm tips include docking hands so two arms can connect together.
- At least 40 sensors plus cameras; current v2 reference manifest defines 48.
- Radiation and thermal shield panels cover fixed body surfaces with functional openings for rails, sensors, radiators, bucket, arms, and rear service.

## What Was Retired From v1

Older values and systems are now historical only unless explicitly reintroduced for trade studies:

- Elevated X-Y-Z gantry.
- Gantry tool bridge and gantry placement accuracy as the primary construction method.
- Helical screw anchors.
- Stabilizer pads, outriggers, and leveling feet.
- Old blade-auger-conveyor intake as the primary excavation method.
- Closed or covered container top.
- Buried batteries/avionics without practical rear service access.
- 70 m3 / 110,000 kg ballast values as fixed v2 numbers; these must be recalculated from the new 8 m x 6 m x 4 m geometry, raised floor, wall thickness, mechanisms, and fill limits.
- 30 kWh battery storage as a fixed v2 value; storage must be resized from the new power and survival model.
- 380 kWh/day full-rate energy demand as a fixed v2 value; energy demand must be recalculated for the bucket/arm architecture.

## Current Project Sentence

CONTAINER v2 is an early-stage lunar construction machine built around an 8 m x 6 m x 4 m open-top regolith container, using a front sliding-lift excavation bucket, four long-reach side-rail robotic arms, distributed sensors/cameras, rear-serviceable underfloor batteries and avionics, and body-wide radiation/thermal protection to collect, handle, place, and use lunar regolith for landing-site construction.

## Phase 1 Focus

Phase 1 models and prototypes must answer:

- Can the v2 machine remain stable without anchors or stabilizers during bucket loading, arm reach, partial fill, slope, and braking?
- What excavation, lift, curl, and rail loads does the front bucket create?
- What reach, payload, actuator torque, and stiffness are realistic for the four long-reach side-rail arms?
- Can two docking arm tips latch, share loads, release, and survive dust contamination?
- What usable regolith volume and working mass remain after raised floor, wall thickness, shield allowance, and mechanism clearances?
- What mobility module can support the body mass and ground pressure at empty, partial-fill, and filled states?
- Can batteries and avionics be cooled and serviced from the protected underfloor rear-access bay?
- Does the 48-sensor/camera layout provide enough coverage around bucket, arms, bin fill state, mobility, thermal, dust, and rear equipment bay?
- Does the v2 architecture still support full pad/berm construction, or should the first demonstrator focus on regolith excavation, collection, handling, and dumping?
